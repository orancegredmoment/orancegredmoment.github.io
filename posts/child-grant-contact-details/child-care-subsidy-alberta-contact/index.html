Who Can Benefit from the Subsidy?#

The child care subsidy program in Alberta is designed to provide financial assistance to eligible families who need help affording high-quality child care services. If you meet the following criteria, you may be eligible for the subsidy:

  • You’re a resident of Alberta with a child under 13.
  • Your child is enrolled in a licensed child care program.
  • You’re working, studying, or participating in training or skills development.
  • Your family income falls within the eligibility limits set by the program.

How Much Assistance Can I Receive?#

The amount of subsidy you receive will depend on various factors, including your family income, the type of child care you’re using, and the number of children you have in care. However, the subsidy can cover a significant portion of your child care costs, making it more affordable for you to access high-quality care.

Applying for the Subsidy: A Step-by-Step Guide#

  1. Gather the Required Documents:

Start by collecting the necessary documents to support your application. These may include:

  • Proof of identity and residency

  1. Complete the Application Form:

Visit the Alberta Child Care Subsidy website to access the application form. Fill it out accurately and completely, ensuring you provide all the required information.

  1. Submit Your Application:

Once you’ve completed the form, submit it along with the supporting documents to your local child care office or agency. You can also submit your application online through the government’s website.

  1. Wait for Processing:

The processing time for subsidy applications can vary depending on your location and the volume of applications being processed. Generally, it can take a few weeks to several months before you receive a decision.

  1. Review the Outcome:

Once your application is processed, you’ll receive notification of the decision. If you’re approved, you’ll receive information on how and when you’ll receive the subsidy payments. If your application is denied, you’ll be provided with the reasons for the denial and information on how to appeal the decision.

How to Apply for the Subsidy and What Documents Are Required?#

Are you a working parent or guardian struggling to afford high-quality child care? The Government of Alberta’s Child Care Subsidy Program is here to help! This program provides financial assistance to eligible families to help cover the cost of child care.

Key Takeaways:#

  • The Child Care Subsidy Program is available to families with low to moderate incomes.
  • The amount of subsidy you receive depends on your family’s income and the cost of child care in your area.
  • You can apply for the subsidy online or by mail.
  • You will need to provide documentation to verify your eligibility, such as proof of income and proof of child care expenses.

How to Apply for the Subsidy#

  1. Determine Your Eligibility:

    • To be eligible for the subsidy, you must meet the following criteria:
      • You must be a resident of Alberta.
      • You must have a child under the age of 13.
      • You must be working, attending school, or participating in a training program.
      • You must have a low to moderate income.

  2. Gather Your Documents:

    • To apply for the subsidy, you will need to provide the following documents:
      • Proof of income (e.g., pay stubs, tax returns, social assistance statements)
      • Proof of child care expenses (e.g., receipts, invoices, contracts)
      • Proof of your child’s age and identity (e.g., birth certificate, passport)
      • Proof of your residency in Alberta (e.g., driver’s license, utility bill)

  3. Complete the Application:

    • You can apply for the subsidy online or by mail. The online application is available on the Government of Alberta’s website. If you are applying by mail, you can download the application form from the website or request a copy by calling the Child Care Subsidy Program office.

  4. Submit Your Application:

    • Once you have completed the application, you can submit it online or by mail. If you are submitting your application by mail, you should send it to the following address:

      Child Care Subsidy Program PO Box 2000 Edmonton, AB T5J 4E1

What Happens After You Apply?#

  • Once you have submitted your application, it will be reviewed by a child care subsidy officer. The officer will assess your eligibility and determine the amount of subsidy you are entitled to receive.
  • If you are approved for the subsidy, you will receive a letter in the mail. The letter will include instructions on how to access your subsidy payments.
  • You will need to continue to provide proof of income and proof of child care expenses on a regular basis. If your circumstances change, you must notify the Child Care Subsidy Program office.
